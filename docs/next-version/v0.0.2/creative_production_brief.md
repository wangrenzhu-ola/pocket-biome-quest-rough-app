# Pocket Biome Quest v0.0.2 Creative Production Brief

## Creative route

**Route name: Neighborhood Almanac / Pocket Field Cabinet**

The visual system should feel like a tiny field cabinet assembled after short neighborhood walks: paper postcards, specimen labels, sidewalk textures, color chips, and gentle hand-drawn habitat marks. It should avoid generic wellness gradients, generic checklist cards, and scientific authority styling.

## Creative-production skill mapping

- `creative-production-explore`: selected next path is product-in-context scenes plus seasonal pack previews, not standalone artwork.
- `creative-production-moodboard`: field cabinet, paper texture, muted habitat swatches, tactile labels.
- `creative-production-scene`: walk-start plan, child/adult noticing roles, weekly almanac recap, premium seasonal preview.
- `creative-production-shot`: top-down postcard/almanac composition and macro habitat details.

## Mood territories

### Territory A — Field Cabinet Calm

- Palette: sage green, warm paper, soil brown, lichen yellow, rain blue, stone gray.
- Surface: translucent paper cards over soft notebook background.
- Motifs: specimen labels, washi-tape corners, tiny habitat stamps.
- Motion: card settles into archive; weekly swatches slide into an almanac strip.
- Use for: Trail Almanac, saved Field Postcards, weekly recap.

### Territory B — Sidewalk Safari Energy

- Palette: moss green + pollen gold accents + asphalt charcoal.
- Surface: quest cards with bolder habitat glyphs and time chips.
- Motifs: tiny arrows, curb-edge lines, magnifier ring, “next tiny stop.”
- Motion: 3-card trail plan fans open before a walk.
- Use for: Micro-Safari Trail Plan and Quest Deck.

### Territory C — Seasonal Pack Theatre

- Palette changes by pack: winter lichen pale green/stone, spring pollinator gold/petal, rain puddle blue/charcoal.
- Surface: locked preview postcards with visible sample quest chain.
- Motifs: seasonal stamp, preview swatches, locked-but-readable sample.
- Motion: pack card flips from value preview to StoreKit state.
- Use for: Premium Quest Packs.

## Product-in-context scenes

| Scene | User situation | UI placement | Creative purpose |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| “Before the block” | User has 10 minutes and needs a simple plan. | Quest Deck → Micro-Safari Trail Plan composer. | Converts vague motivation into a walk-ready sequence. |
| “One spots, one tells” | Parent-child pair shares a walk. | Observation Capture role prompt toggle. | Makes the app feel social without accounts/community. |
| “Pocket field cabinet” | User opens archive after several walks. | Trail Almanac weekly recap. | Turns saved notes into identity and memory, not records. |
| “Try the season” | User considers premium pack. | Premium preview card. | Shows value before purchase without blocking free flow. |

## Shot direction

### Hero shot: “Three tiny habitats, one short walk”

- Crop: top-down notebook with three overlapping quest cards.
- Elements: moss chip, bark line, puddle reflection swatch.
- UI placement: Micro-Safari Trail Plan header.
- Constraint: do not imply map/GPS or live route tracking.

### Detail shot: “Postcard becomes almanac”

- Crop: saved field postcard sliding into a weekly strip.
- Elements: habitat glyph stamp, color chips, date label.
- UI placement: Trail Almanac empty/success/recap states.
- Constraint: no fake community likes, rankings, or species labels.

### Premium shot: “Seasonal quest pack shelf”

- Crop: three small pack cards as cabinet drawers.
- Elements: winter lichen, spring pollinator, rain puddle marks.
- UI placement: Premium Quest Packs.
- Constraint: preview must be honest; locked content visible as sample, not usable unless purchased.

## Avoid list

- No purple/blue AI-gradient hero.
- No generic checklist/bingo grid as primary UI.
- No species certainty, diagnosis, expert, community, leaderboard, or live recommendation visual language.
- No map pins or route lines unless GPS/location is explicitly implemented in a future approved version.
- No internal design-process copy in user-facing screens.
